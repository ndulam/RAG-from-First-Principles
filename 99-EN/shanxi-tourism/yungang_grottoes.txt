Yungang Grottoes

The Yungang Grottoes are located at the southern foot of Wuzhou Mountain, about 17 kilometers west of Datong in northern Shanxi Province, China. The caves were carved into the mountain and extend for roughly one kilometer from east to west. The site contains 45 major caves, 252 niches of various sizes, and more than 51,000 stone statues. It is one of China's largest ancient cave temple complexes and is often mentioned alongside Dunhuang Mogao Caves, Longmen Grottoes, and Maijishan Grottoes as a major treasure house of Buddhist cave art. The site was listed among China's first major protected cultural heritage sites in 1961, inscribed on the UNESCO World Heritage List on December 14, 2001, and rated as one of China's first national 5A tourist attractions on May 8, 2007.

Wuhua Caves

The Wuhua Caves are Caves 9 to 13 in the central section of the Yungang Grottoes. Their name comes from the clay coloring applied during the Qing dynasty. The caves are richly decorated and brightly colored. They are important sources for studying Northern Wei history, art, music, dance, calligraphy, and architecture.

Pagoda Caves

The eastern caves of Yungang, Caves 1 to 4, are pagoda-style caves. Caves 1 and 2 were excavated as a pair before Emperor Xiaowen moved the Northern Wei capital to Luoyang. Each cave has a square central pagoda pillar with niches on four sides. Cave 1 features Maitreya as the main image, with reliefs of Sakyamuni and Prabhutaratna below and Sakyamuni above on the south side of the pillar. A five-story relief pagoda provides valuable visual evidence for Northern Wei architecture. Cave 2 features Sakyamuni, three Buddhas, and carved images of Vimalakirti and Manjusri near the south wall entrance. Cave 3 is the largest cave at Yungang, with a front cliff wall about 25 meters high. It is traditionally associated with the Tanyao translation hall.

Wuzhou Mountain

Wuzhou Mountain, also known as Wuzhou Shan, lies west of Datong. The Song dynasty text Taiping Huanyu Ji cites Jizhou Tu as saying that the mountain stretches hundreds of li from east to west and about fifty li from north to south, with a steep southern face. The Yungang Grottoes were carved into the southern edge of this mountain.

Tanyao Five Caves

Caves 16 to 20 are the earliest group at Yungang and are known as the Tanyao Five Caves. Cave 16 has an oval plan. Its central statue is Sakyamuni, 13.5 meters high, standing on a lotus pedestal. The surrounding walls are carved with a thousand Buddhas and many niches. Cave 17 contains images of the Buddhas of the three times, with a central seated Maitreya statue 15.6 meters high. Additional niches appear on the east and west walls. A niche dated to the thirteenth year of the Taihe reign of the Northern Wei dynasty, or 489 CE, was added later.

Address

The Yungang Grottoes are located at the southern foot of Wuzhou Mountain, about 17 kilometers west of Datong, Shanxi Province.

Best Time to Visit

May to October.

Opening Hours

8:30 to 17:30 from April 1 to October 15, and 8:30 to 17:00 from October 16 to March 31. The Yungang Museum is open from 9:30 to 17:00 from April 1 to October 15, and from 9:30 to 16:20 from October 16 to March 31.

Tickets

Peak season, April 1 to October 15: 125 yuan, with half-price tickets at 60 yuan. Low season, October 16 to March 31: 80 yuan, with half-price tickets at 40 yuan.

Transportation

Buses and tourist coaches to Yungang are available from the west gate area of Datong. From Datong Railway Station, take Bus 4 to the Xinkaili bus station in the western part of the city, then take Bus 3 directly to the east gate of the scenic area. Minibuses from the railway station toward Yanshan also stop near the site.

Visitor Notes

Information phone: 0352-3206805. Electric carts inside the scenic area cost 10 yuan per person. Free admission is available for eligible seniors, young children, disabled visitors, disabled veterans, active-duty military personnel, and qualified guides with valid documents. Half-price tickets are available for eligible minors and full-time students with valid identification.
