Black Myth: Wukong

Black Myth: Wukong is an action role-playing game developed by Game Science. It is inspired by Journey to the West, one of the Four Great Classical Novels of Chinese literature. The game was released for Windows and PlayStation 5 on August 20, 2024, with an Xbox Series X/S version planned for a later date.

The game is commonly described as one of China's first major AAA-style single-player titles. Before launch, it attracted attention for its combat system, art direction, and use of Chinese mythological material. After launch, it quickly reached the top of several sales charts and became one of the fastest-selling games of its period.

Gameplay

The player controls a monkey warrior known as the Destined One. The main weapon is a staff inspired by the Ruyi Jingu Bang. Combat includes several staff stances, including smash, thrust, and pillar styles. Players manage stamina and focus while combining light attacks, heavy attacks, dodges, and charged moves.

The game also includes several spell categories. Immobilize can briefly freeze an enemy. Cloud Step allows the player to leave a decoy and reposition. A Pluck of Many summons duplicates that assist in combat. Transformations let the protagonist take on different forms, each with its own attacks and health. Spirits obtained from defeated enemies provide additional combat abilities.

Progression is mostly linear, but many areas allow exploration. Shrines serve as checkpoints. The world changes as the story advances, and enemies include demons, beasts, and major bosses. New Game Plus adds additional challenge and unlocks further abilities and secrets.

Setting

The story is divided into six chapters: Black Cloud, Yellow Wind, White Snow, Purple Web, Flaming Mountains, and Unfinished. The game has two endings. Each chapter ends with animated sequences that expand the narrative and themes.

The visual design draws from Chinese landmarks and religious art, including Dazu Rock Carvings in Chongqing and many sites in Shanxi, such as Xiaoxitian, Nanchan Temple, Tiefo Temple, Guangsheng Temple, Yungang Grottoes, and the Stork Tower. Buddhist and Taoist imagery appear throughout the world.

Story

The story takes place after the events of Journey to the West. In the old monkey's tale, Sun Wukong rejects heavenly restraint after attaining Buddhahood and attempts to return to Mount Huaguo. Heaven suspects his rebellion and sends Erlang Shen, the Four Heavenly Kings, and other heavenly forces against him. During the battle, the old headband curse reappears. Wukong is defeated, his body becomes a stone egg, and his spirit is divided into six relics.

Years later, the Destined One leaves Mount Huaguo to recover these relics and uncover the truth behind Wukong's fate. The journey takes him through Black Wind Mountain, Yellow Wind Ridge, the snowy lands of New West, Webbed Hollow, the Flaming Mountains, and optional paths connected to Erlang Shen. Depending on the player's discoveries, the final confrontation can either continue the cycle of control or allow Wukong's will to return.

Development

Game Science began development in 2017 and announced the project with a gameplay trailer in 2020. Early trailers showed exploration, boss battles, and environments inspired by Chinese temples, grottoes, and mountain landscapes. Later demonstrations used Unreal Engine 5.

The development team scanned real-world architecture, sculptures, and religious sites to build the game's environments. Many locations in Shanxi influenced the final art direction, including temples, grottoes, pagodas, and historic buildings.

Editions

The game launched in several editions. The Digital Standard Edition includes the base game. The Digital Deluxe Edition adds in-game equipment and a selected digital soundtrack. Physical Deluxe and Collector's editions include activation codes and collectible items such as a steel case, scrolls, jewelry-style accessories, badges, postcards, and a figure in the Collector's Edition.

Reception

The game received strong attention for its visual quality, cultural adaptation, and action combat. Reviewers praised its ambition and presentation, while noting that it still reflected the constraints of a relatively young studio. Its release was widely viewed as an important moment for Chinese single-player game development and for the international visibility of Chinese mythology in games.
